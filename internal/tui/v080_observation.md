## Ledger A — Spatial Hierarchy (Observed)

### Ready

| Size | Body lines | Content lines | Dead lines | Ratio |
|------|-----------|--------------|------------|-------|
| 80×24 | 19 | 7 | 12 | 37% dead |
| 100×30 | 25 | 7 | 18 | 72% dead |
| 120×40 | 35 | 7 | 28 | 80% dead |
| 160×40 | 35 | 7 | 28 | 80% dead |

Content order: CTA → URL → CC → action links. All vertically centered via lipgloss.Place.

Observation: At 80×24 the dead space ratio is tolerable (centering needs room). At 100×30+ the dead space consumes the majority of the body. The centered layout produces a narrow ribbon of content floating in blank space. At 160×40 this effect is extreme — the action links ("e Endpoint c Concurrency p Payload") are 25 lines away from the separator above and 14 lines from the status bar below. The spatial hierarchy has collapsed: blank space is the dominant visual element at large sizes.

### TimelineRunning

| Size | Body lines | Content lines | Dead lines | Ratio |
|------|-----------|--------------|------------|-------|
| 80×24 | 19 | 19 (identity + 17 visible rows) | 0 | 0% |
| 100×30 | 25 | 22 (identity + metrics + 20 rows) | 3 | 12% |
| 120×40 | 35 | 22 (identity + metrics + 20 rows) | 13 | 37% |
| 160×40 | 35 | 22 (identity + metrics + 20 rows) | 13 | 37% |

Observation: At 80×24 the surface is perfectly filled — no dead space. At 100×30 all 20 results fit with minimal waste. At 120×40+ the results are exhausted and blank space accumulates below. The horizontal scaling is the real concern: barWidth grows from 42 chars (80 cols) to 122 chars (160 cols). The bar outgrows its purpose as a visual indicator and becomes a wall of fill characters.

### LogsRunning

| Size | Body lines | Content lines | Dead lines | Ratio |
|------|-----------|--------------|------------|-------|
| 80×24 | 19 | 19 | 0 | 0% |
| 100×30 | 25 | 22 | 3 | 12% |
| 120×40 | 35 | 22 | 13 | 37% |
| 160×40 | 35 | 22 | 13 | 37% |

Observation: Same content/density as TimelineRunning but without the bar scaling problem. Logs add URL text instead of bars, which naturally fills horizontal space. The extra width at 160×40 is absorbed by longer URL display. Logs is the most graceful surface across sizes — no pathological scaling.

### TimelineRunningEmpty (Transitional)

| Size | Body lines | Content lines | Dead lines | Ratio |
|------|-----------|--------------|------------|-------|
| 80×24 | 19 | 3 (identity, metrics, empty msg) | 16 | 84% |
| 100×30 | 25 | 3 | 22 | 88% |
| 120×40 | 35 | 3 | 32 | 91% |
| 160×40 | 35 | 3 | 32 | 91% |

Observation: The worst dead-space ratio of all surfaces. Three content lines in a 35-line body. The metrics display ("0% ok • 0.0 r/s • p90 0.00s • p99 0.00s") is technically correct but visually meaningless — an empty run cannot produce meaningful metrics. The status bar advertises "↑↓ • Enter" which are inert (no results to navigate). This surfaces a design question: should transitional states show metrics at all, or only after the first result arrives?

### Inspect

| Size | Body lines | Content lines | Dead lines | Ratio |
|------|-----------|--------------|------------|-------|
| 80×24 | 19 | 12 (identity + meta + headers + body) | 7 | 37% |
| 100×30 | 25 | 12 | 13 | 52% |
| 120×40 | 35 | 12 | 23 | 66% |
| 160×40 | 35 | 12 | 23 | 66% |

Observation: Content is adequate at 80×24. At larger sizes the content stays fixed while dead space accumulates. Inspect has the most structured content (multiple sections with blank-line separators) so the blank space between sections is intentional breathing room, not waste. The dead space at the bottom (below the body section) is true waste.

### Payload

| Size | Body lines | Content lines | Dead lines | Ratio |
|------|-----------|--------------|------------|-------|
| 80×24 | 19 | 9 | 10 | 53% |
| 160×40 | 35 | 9 | 26 | 74% |

Observation: Compact form at all sizes. The headers list and body input are functional but sparse. At 160×40 the form occupies the top 10 lines of a 35-line body — 25 lines of dead space below. Payload does not accept a height parameter so it cannot adapt.

### Endpoint

| Size | Body lines | Content lines | Dead lines | Ratio |
|------|-----------|--------------|------------|-------|
| 80×24 | 19 | 5 | 14 | 74% |
| 160×40 | 35 | 5 | 30 | 86% |

Observation: The most compact dialog after Concurrency. URL input + method selector = 5 content lines. At 80×24 it is already sparse. At 160×40 it is extreme (86% dead space). The method selector row with all 7 methods fits even at 80 cols.

### Concurrency

| Size | Body lines | Content lines | Dead lines | Ratio |
|------|-----------|--------------|------------|-------|
| 80×24 | 19 | 2 | 17 | 89% |
| 160×40 | 35 | 2 | 33 | 94% |

Observation: Highest dead-space ratio of all surfaces. Two content lines (identity + CC value) in a 35-line body. At 94% dead space this is the most obvious candidate for spatial redesign.

### ConfirmQuit

Observations match TimelineRunning since the body is preserved. Only the status bar changes. The quit prompt ("q/Ctrl+C again to quit, any key cancels") replaces the normal mode+ hints. At 160×40 the prompt is a single line in the status bar at the very bottom — easy to miss if the operator is scanning the body content.

## Ledger B — Attention Hierarchy (Observed)

### Eye landing order per surface

| Surface | First target | Second target | Third target |
|---------|-------------|---------------|-------------|
| Ready | Top bar (method+URL+CC) | CTA ("Press Ctrl+R to run") | URL line |
| TimelineRunning | Top bar | Timeline identity | Metrics line |
| LogsRunning | Top bar | Logs identity | URL content in rows |
| TimelineRunningEmpty | Top bar | Timeline identity | Empty-state message |
| Inspect | Top bar | Inspector identity | Method/URL line |
| Payload | Top bar | Payload identity | HEADERS section |
| Endpoint | Top bar | Endpoint identity | URL input |
| Concurrency | Top bar | Concurrency identity | CC value |
| ConfirmQuit | Top bar | Timeline identity | Status bar (quit prompt is third) |

Observation: The top bar is the consistent first visual target on every surface — it has the highest density of information (method, URL, CC) and the bold style. This is intentional: the Context layer is designed to be the persistent state frame.

The identity line (Timeline, Logs, Inspector, or dialog name) is the second target — bold+accent makes it visually distinct from content.

The quit prompt in ConfirmQuit is third, not second — the body content (Timeline rows) has more visual weight than the status bar. This is by design (ConfirmQuit preserves workspace) but means a distracted operator may not notice the quit dialog is open.

### What disappears

- **Ready action links**: "e Endpoint c Concurrency p Payload" at the bottom of a mostly-blank body. Easy to overlook.
- **Metrics in large terminals**: At 160×40, the metrics line is small text (muted style) in a wide blank field. The operator must search for it.
- **Empty-state message in TimelineRunningEmpty**: "⏳ Waiting for results..." is a single line in 32 blank lines at 160×40.
- **Status bar mode badge in ConfirmQuit**: The quit prompt has no mode badge — the entire status bar is plain text. At 160×40 the status bar content is a tiny fraction of the total render.

### What dominates

- **Top bar at all sizes**: Bold text, method+URL+CC is the densest information block.
- **Timeline bars at 160×40**: 122-char block character bars dominate each row visually, overwhelming the status code and latency text.
- **Blank space on dialog surfaces**: At large sizes, the dominant visual element on Concurrency, Endpoint, and Payload is empty space.

## Ledger C — Visual Language (Confirmed)

The existing audit's findings remain correct:

- Accent (#38bdf8 sky blue) / muted (#a1a1aa gray) / error (#f87171 red) palette is consistent.
- Identity = bold + accent. Content = normal. Hints = muted. Errors = danger red. Selection = accent + dark background.
- The system is minimal. No unnecessary decoration.
- Ready has no identity line by design (it is the launch state, not a workspace).

No new visual-language issues found. The palette is sparse but coherent.

## Ledger D — Density (Updated with transitional surface)

Binary behavior confirmed across all surfaces:

- **Fits at 80×24**: TimelineRunning, LogsRunning, Inspect (marginal), Payload (marginal).
- **Does not fit at 80×24** (in the sense of filling the body): Ready, Endpoint, Concurrency, TimelineRunningEmpty.
- **Adaptive**: Only Timeline bars (linear width scaling). No adaptive density mechanism.

New finding from transitional surface: TimelineRunningEmpty has the worst density of any surface — 3 content lines in a 35-line body at 160×40. The empty-state design assumes results will arrive, but the operator sees nothing during the transition. Mitigation: either hide metrics until first result, or add a "waiting" visual (spinner, elapsed time counter).

## Ledger E2 — Responsive Design / Cross-Size Migration

This is the structural evidence for v0.8.1+ decisions.

### What grows with size

| Element | Grows? | Behavior |
|---------|--------|----------|
| Blank body space | Yes | Linear with height. Dead space on Ready, dialogs, empty states. |
| Timeline bar width | Yes | Linear with width. 42 chars at 80, 122 chars at 160. |
| Logs URL space | Yes | Absorbs extra width naturally. No pathological behavior. |
| Separator line | Yes | Dashed line expands to full width. Expected. |
| Top bar URL truncation | Yes | More URL visible at larger widths. Correct. |

### What stays fixed

| Element | Fixed at | Why |
|---------|----------|-----|
| Ready content | 7 lines (50 chars wide) | Static fmt.Sprintf + lipgloss.Place centering |
| Metrics line | 1 line | Fixed format string |
| Dialog content | 2-9 lines | No height parameter accepted |
| Inspect content | ~12 lines | Fixed section structure |
| Empty-state message | 1 line | Single format string |
| Identity line | 1 line | Single format string |

### What breaks first

| Surface | Breaks at | What breaks | Severity |
|---------|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Ready | 100×30 | Dead space exceeds content area | Important |
| Concurrency | 80×24 | 89% dead space at smallest size | Important |
| Timeline bars | 120×40 | Bar width exceeds 80 chars, becomes visually overwhelming | Important |
| TimelineRunningEmpty | 80×24 | 84% dead space at smallest size | Observation |
| Endpoint | 80×24 | 74% dead space at smallest size | Observation |
| Inspect | 120×40 | Content-to-blank ratio inverts | Observation |

### What becomes dominant at large size

- **Blank space**: On Ready, dialogs, and empty states. At 160×40, blank space is the dominant visual element.
- **Timeline bars**: At 160×40, bars are 122 chars of repeating block characters. The latency signal is lost in visual noise.

### What becomes invisible at large size

- **Dialog content**: At 160×40, the compact dialog forms are a small island in a large terminal. The operator must scan to find them.
- **Empty-state messages**: Single-line messages in 30+ line bodies are easily overlooked.
- **Status bar hints**: At 160×40, the status bar is 1-2 lines at the bottom of a 40-line render. Low-contrast muted text on dark background.

## Ledger F — Professionalism (Updated)

Existing findings confirmed:

- 80×24 experience is clean and operational.
- 160×40 experience shows architecture limits of linear scaling.
- The v0.7.x "remove chrome" mandate produced a correct but visually primitive baseline.

New observations:

- **TimelineRunningEmpty metrics**: "0% ok • 0.0 r/s • p90 0.00s • p99 0.00s" is displayed before any results arrive. This is mathematically correct but presents meaningless data to the operator. A transitional state should either hide metrics or show them as dashes/placeholders.
- **Inert status bar hints on transitional state**: "↑↓ • Enter" advertised when no results exist to scroll. This is the same class of bug that v0.7.9 fixed for Ready, now present on the transitional surface.
- **All-dialogs-dead-space**: The three dialogs (Endpoint, Concurrency, Payload) are architecturally identical in their problem: compact forms that don't accept a height parameter. This is a consistent pattern, not an exception.

## Ledger G — Canonicalization

### Ownership

| Concern | Owner | Quality |
|---------|-------|---------|
| Context (top bar) | `renderTopBar` | Clean. Owns method display, URL, CC. No overlap. |
| Identity | `identityCell` helper | Clean. Used by Timeline, Logs, Inspector, all dialogs. Single source. |
| Metrics | `metricsString()` + `renderResultList` | Shared. metricsString is called by renderResultList. No duplication. |
| Result rows | `renderResultList` + row function | Clean abstraction. Timeline and Logs pass different rowFn. |
| Empty state | `renderEmptyState` | Clean. Handles running vs idle vs no-URL cases. |
| Dialogs | Each dialog owns its render | Endpoint, Concurrency, Payload are independent. No shared dialog infrastructure. |
| Interaction | `renderStatusBar` | Clean switch/case dispatch. Single source for all states. |

### Ownership concentration (knows too much)

**renderReady** (`view.go:95-105`):
This is the most concentrated surface. A single `fmt.Sprintf` and `lipgloss.Place` call owns:
- Content text (CTA wording)
- Content layout (all lines in one string)
- Spacing (via lipgloss.Place centering)
- Positioning (center/center anchor)

Every concern — what to show, how to arrange it, where to place it — is in one call. If Ready is redesigned for spatial refinement (v0.8.1 candidate), this must be decomposed.

**renderInspect** (`view.go:353-419`):
Builds a `[]string` slice for all content sections. Owns:
- Metadata section (method, URL, status, latency)
- Headers section (sorting, rendering, truncation)
- Body section (line splitting, truncation, overflow)

Moderate concentration. Headers and body are separate concerns but have no separate helpers. Acceptable at this scale.

### Structural exceptions

| Exception | Surface | Why it's exceptional | Justified? |
|-----------|---------|---------------------|------------|
| No identity | Ready | Launch state has no workspace identity | Yes — design decision |
| No body renderer | ConfirmQuit | Preserves underlying workspace | Yes — interaction overlay |
| Read-only | Inspect | Only surface with no selection cursor | Yes — detail view |
| No height parameter | Dialogs | Endpoint, CC, Payload don't accept height | Partially — creates dead space at large sizes |

### Style definition duplication

| Location | What | Status |
|----------|------|--------|
| `renderInspect` | `muted` style created fresh per call | Minor duplication. Could be module-level var. |
| `renderInspect` | `errorText` style created fresh per call | Minor duplication. Could be module-level var. |
| `renderInspect` | `sectionLine` style created fresh per call | Minor duplication. Could be module-level var. |
| `renderStatusBar` | `hintsStyled` uses inline muted style | Minor. Not worth extracting. |
| `renderReady` | Inline `Foreground(lipgloss.Color(colorText))` | Already uses named constant. Fine. |
| `rowStyle` / `errorRowStyle` | Module-level functions | Correct — shared. |
| `identityCell` | Module-level function | Correct — shared. |

Finding: No meaningful style duplication. The three inline styles in renderInspect are the only candidates for consolidation. Low priority (defer to v0.8.3 visual language pass).

### Test duplication

| Test | What it checks | Overlap with v080_audit tests |
|------|---------------|-------------------------------|
| `TestView_Idle` | Content strings present | None — checks Ready content, not width/height |
| `TestView_Running` | Content strings present | None — checks running state content |
| `TestRenderStatusBar_*` | Mode labels present | None — string content checks |
| `TestV080Audit_AllSurfaces` | Width/height contracts | Novel — no existing test checks line counts or visual widths |
| `TestV080Audit_E1VerticalPadding` | Line-by-line width at 80×24 | Novel — specific to Ready 80×24 |
| `TestV080Audit_E1StyleBleed` | Width across all sizes | Novel — cross-size width/height validation |

Finding: No meaningful test duplication. The E1 checks are a new class of validation (renderer contract guarantees) that complement the existing content-presence tests. Each serves a different purpose: "does it render the right thing" vs "does it render correctly."

### Timeline/Logs shared logic

`renderResultList` is shared. Timeline and Logs differ only in:
- Identity string ("Timeline" vs "Logs")
- Empty-state message ("⏳ Waiting..." vs "📭 No results yet...")
- Row function (bar rendering vs URL rendering)

This is correctly abstracted. No duplication. The abstraction boundary is at the row function level, which is appropriate.

### Audit infrastructure ownership

The new `audit.go` file owns:
- `AuditSurface` type
- `AuditSizes` list
- `AllAuditSurfaces()` — authoritative surface list
- `WriteAuditCapture()` — capture generation

The test file owns:
- `checkE1` — renderer integrity validation
- All Test* functions — test execution + assertion

This separation is clean: audit infrastructure vs audit validation. The test file does not generate files. The script (`scripts/audit/main.go`) generates captures.

## Ledger H — Cohesion

### Question: Do Ready, Timeline, Inspect feel related?

Partially. All surfaces share:
- Top bar (same format)
- Separators (same style)
- Status bar (same dispatch pattern with mode badge)
- Background (same dark theme)
- Base font (same terminal default)

But the body layouts differ significantly:
- Ready uses centered `lipgloss.Place` — the only surface to do so.
- Timeline/Logs use a left-aligned list with identity + metrics + rows.
- Inspect uses a structured document with sections and headers.
- Dialogs use compact forms with identity + input fields.

The centering in Ready is a visual outlier — no other surface centers its content. This is the architectural source of the "Ready feels different" observation.

### Question: Do dialogs feel from the same visual family?

Yes, with caveats:
- All three dialogs (Endpoint, Concurrency, Payload) use the same identity style (muted, not bold+accent like workspace surfaces).
- Endpoint and Concurrency have compact single-field forms. Payload is more complex (headers table + body input).
- Payload has editing affordances (ctrl+n add, ctrl+d remove) that Endpoint and Concurrency do not — but this is a functional difference, not a style difference.
- No dialog uses a visual frame/border — the separator above and below is the only structural cue. This is consistent.

### Question: Is identity language consistent?

Yes:
- Workspace surfaces (Timeline, Logs) = bold + accent ✅
- Inspector = bold + accent + "Result #N" suffix ✅
- Dialog surfaces (Endpoint, Concurrency, Payload) = muted identity ✅
- Ready = no identity (by design) ✅

### Question: Are separators used consistently?

Yes. All surfaces use the same separator:
```go
StyleSeparator.Render(strings.Repeat("─", width))
```

Placed at line 2 (after top bar) and line -2 (before status bar). Consistent across all surfaces.

### Question: Is whitespace philosophy consistent?

**No — this is a cohesion gap.**

- Ready uses `lipgloss.Place` center/center anchoring — dynamic whitespace distribution.
- Dialogs use no anchoring — they render compactly at the top and leave blank space below.
- Timeline/Logs use `StyleBase.Width(width).Height(height).Render(...)` — fills remaining space with the base background.
- Inspect uses `StyleBase.Copy().Width(width).Height(height).Render(strings.Join(...))` — same as Timeline/Logs.

There are three different whitespace strategies:
1. **Dynamic centering** (Ready)
2. **Compact top** (Dialogs)
3. **Fill remaining** (Timeline, Logs, Inspect)

This is a cohesion gap. A consistent whitespace philosophy would apply the same anchoring or distribution to all surfaces.

### Question: Is the status bar consistent across states?

Yes — the same `renderStatusBar` dispatches correctly for all states. The mode badge (OBSERVE, RUNNING, INSPECTING, ENDPOINT, CONCURRENCY, PAYLOAD) is the first element, followed by hints. ConfirmQuit is the only exception (no mode badge, plain text). This exception is justified — quit confirmation is an urgent interaction that should be visually distinct.

### Cohesion summary

| Aspect | Consistent? | Gap |
|--------|------------|-----|
| Top bar | Yes | — |
| Separators | Yes | — |
| Status bar | Yes | — |
| Identity language | Yes | — |
| Body layout philosophy | **No** | Three different strategies (center, compact-top, fill-remaining) |
| Dialog family resemblance | Partial | Payload is more complex but same style |
| Color palette | Yes | — |
| Selection highlighting | Yes | Accent + dark background everywhere |

The primary cohesion gap is the body layout philosophy — different surfaces use different anchoring strategies. This is likely what produces the "medieval" or "feels off" perception: the operator subconsciously registers that surfaces behave differently in space even though styling is consistent.

## Direction Decision (Updated)

Based on all ledgers (A–H), the v0.8.x roadmap is:

### Primary — v0.8.1: Spatial Design Pass

Focus on the surfaces with the most severe spatial problems:

| Priority | Surface | Problem | Evidence |
|----------|---------|---------|----------|
| P0 | Ready | Centered layout is an outlier; dead space at >80×24 | Ledger A (37-80% dead), Ledger H (centering is the only surface using Place) |
| P0 | Concurrency | 94% dead space at 160×40 | Ledger A, Ledger E2 |
| P1 | Endpoint | 86% dead space at 160×40 | Ledger A, Ledger E2 |
| P1 | Payload | 74% dead space, no height adaptation | Ledger A, Ledger G (no height parameter) |
| P1 | Timeline bars | 122-char bar blocks at 160×40 | Ledger E2 (pathological width scaling) |
| P2 | Inspect | Content-to-blank ratio inverts at 120×40+ | Ledger A |

Design questions to answer:
1. Should Ready use an upper-third anchor instead of geometric center?
2. Should dialogs accept a height parameter and distribute content?
3. Should Timeline bars have an absolute max width (~60 chars)?
4. Should the status bar have a max width (cap at ~60 chars)?

### Secondary — v0.8.2: Cohesion Pass

| Issue | Ledger reference |
|-------|-----------------|
| Unify body layout philosophy across all surfaces | Ledger H |
| Standardize whitespace strategy (all compact-top, all centered, or all fill-remaining) | Ledger H |
| Resolve dialog family hierarchy (why are dialogs muted-identity while workspaces are bold-identity?) | Ledger H, Ledger C |
| Make empty/transitional states feel intentional rather than leftover | Ledger D, Ledger F |

### Later — v0.8.3: Visual Language

After layout and cohesion are stable:

| Issue | Ledger reference |
|-------|-----------------|
| Style var consolidation (renderInspect inline styles) | Ledger G |
| Expand palette for secondary accents | Ledger C |
| Typography hierarchy (if terminal supports) | — |
| Consider subtle borders for dialog surfaces | Ledger H |

### Decision

**Mixed refinement** — spatial design is the primary weakness. Cohesion is secondary. Visual language is deferred.

Renderer integrity (E1) is confirmed healthy across all 9 surfaces × 4 sizes. The architecture is production-worthy for correctness. The remaining work is user-experience refinement.

The transitional surface (TimelineRunningEmpty) surfaced a previously unknown interaction issue: metrics are displayed before any results arrive, and the status bar advertises inert controls. This should be corrected before v0.8.1 (follows the v0.7.9 precedent).
